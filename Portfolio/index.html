Poetry

Not Alone

Here we go again,
I think as I hear, in the middle of the night,
Scratch, scratch, scratch
My dog's claws sharply scraping the floor,
right above my head.

Scratch, scratch, scratch,
The poor things paces some more
I start to get up,
So she's not alone.
When suddenly, she stops moving.

I hear a tiny thump,
As she lays down.
Hopefully for the rest of the night.
Until this whole thing
Begins once more tomorrow.

Persisting Predation

It comes out of nowhere,
Feeling like a snake wrapped around my chest.
Squeezing me, trapping me,
Poisioning me with doubts.
Feeding on my misery.

First it's just a feeling
Then it winds me up like a toy,
And lets me spiral into a panic.
Only to repeat the process later.
It is always there, lurking,
Watching everything, a predator stalking prey,
Waiting for its moment to strike.
It never fails to find that one moment on an otherwise perfect day.

Is it trying to communicate with me,
To warn me of something,
Or is its only purpose to torment and haunt?
I wish it would tell me.
Instead it goes silent once more,
Waiting for its next moment to attack.
